E-Farmer

BackGround

E-farmer is an application that aggregates all the existing agricultural technologies for easy access by the farmers. Several technological solutions exist in the agricultural sector but a large number of farmers are not aware of these technologies or the means of accessing them.

My Role

    Conduct user research.

    Design the interface.

    Prototyping.

    Iterating the design.

Duration and Procedure

December 2021 - January 2022.
The procedure involved was collecting data to see if the solution will be of help to the farmers. The data was collected and anallyzed by a team member who we were working with in this project.

Progress and Future Steps

The application is now in the final design stages where we have the high fidelity prototype ready for the display.

Persona

Methodology

Unmoderated usability study.

Location: Kenya (the participants will do the study remotely via Google forms).

Sessions will take place remotely any time a user has access to the form.

Five or more participants will undertake the study completing various tasks on the flow: sign up, login, use of search button, selection of a value chain.

The user has about 45 minutes to complete the session.

Participants

Participants are farmers who have access to Internet connection, a smart phone, and who use or want to start using technology to boost their agricultural activites.

Scripts

Prompt 1: Sign Up

  Prompt 1 Follow-up: How easy was it to create your profile?

  Did you encounter any challenge in the process?

  How long did it take you?

Prompt 2: Select a value addition chain.

  Were you able to find what you wanted?

  How can we improve that procedure?

  How fast were you able to do that?

UX Research Study Plan

Title and Purpose

E-Farmer is an application seeking to bring the various services that farmers need into a central place. The results will be faster access and having a variety from which the users can choose from.

Research Goals

The major research goal is to find out how many farmers can be interested in an application that brings them such services together and decreasing the time they spend finding those from other sources.

Key Performance Indicators

Time on task.

User error rates.

Conversion rates.

Turnaround time.
